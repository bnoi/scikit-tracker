For details about link_percentile, see below from K. Jaqaman and G. Danuser, Nature Methods, 2008.

TFA: For track segment ends and starts, the alternative cost (b and d in Fig. 1c) had to be comparable in magnitude to the costs of potential assignments, making the rejection of gap closing, merging and splitting an accessible alternative. At the same time, the alternative cost had to be at the higher end of the range of potential assignment costs, so that the algorithm did not fail to close gaps and capture merge and split events. We performed empirical tests of the sensitivity of tracking results to variations in the alternative cost. We found that in a range 80th – 100th percentile of all potential assignment costs the outcome of gap closing, merging and splitting varied negligibly (data not shown). We attribute this robustness to the fact that track initiations and terminations competed globally, in space and time, with all other potential assignments. Thus, the alternative cost was taken as the 90th percentile.
